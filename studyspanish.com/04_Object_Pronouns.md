## Direct Object Pronouns (ODPs)

### What Is a Direct Object?

A **direct object** receives the action of the verb.

Examples:

- Bill hit the ball.
  ‚Üí **"ball"** is the direct object.
- Sherry reads the book.
  ‚Üí **"book"** is the direct object.
- Sherry hit Bill.
  ‚Üí **"Bill"** is the direct object.

üí° It answers **"what?"** or **"whom?"**:

- Sherry hit **whom**? ‚Üí Bill
- Bill hit **what**? ‚Üí the ball

> **üîπ Why Use Direct Object Pronouns?**
>
> To avoid repetition:
>
> ‚ùå Paul bought the flowers. He gave **the flowers** to his wife.  
> ‚úÖ Paul bought the flowers. He gave **them** to his wife.

## Indirect Object Pronouns (IOPs)

### What Is an Indirect Object?

- The **indirect object (IO)** tells **to whom** or **for whom** the action of the verb is done.
- It indicates **where the direct object (DO)** is going.

Examples:

- He gives **the book** (DO) **to Mar√≠a** (IO).
- She buys **flowers** (DO) **for me** (IO).

> ‚ùó IO usually appears **with** a DO, even if the DO is implied:
>
> - ‚ÄúMy mom writes me (a letter).‚Äù  
> - ‚ÄúShe told him (the news).‚Äù
