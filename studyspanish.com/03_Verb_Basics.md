# Verb Basics

## 🔹 What is a Verb?

- A verb expresses **action**.
  - Examples: *run, sit, eat, swim, study*
- The **infinitive** is the base form of a verb:
  - English: *to run, to swim*
  - Spanish: *hablar (to speak), comer (to eat), vivir (to live)*

## 🔹 Regular Verbs

### Three Types of Spanish Verbs

All Spanish verbs are either **regular** or **irregular**. Regular verbs follow predictable patterns based on their infinitive endings:

- **-ar verbs** (like *hablar* - to speak)
- **-er verbs** (like *comer* - to eat)  
- **-ir verbs** (like *vivir* - to live)

### How to Conjugate Regular Verbs

**Step 1: Drop the Infinitive Ending**

Remove the -ar, -er, or -ir from the end of the verb.

**Step 2: Add the Appropriate Ending**

The ending depends on:

1. The **subject** (who is doing the action)
2. The **verb type** (-ar, -er, or -ir)

### Present Tense Conjugation Patterns

**-ar Verbs (hablar - to speak)**

| Subject Pronoun  | Ending | Example  |
| ---------------- | ------ | -------- |
| yo               | -o     | hablo    |
| tú               | -as    | hablas   |
| él/ella/Ud.      | -a     | habla    |
| nosotros/as      | -amos  | hablamos |
| vosotros/as      | -áis   | habláis  |
| ellos/ellas/Uds. | -an    | hablan   |

**-er Verbs (comer - to eat)**

| Subject Pronoun  | Ending | Example |
| ---------------- | ------ | ------- |
| yo               | -o     | como    |
| tú               | -es    | comes   |
| él/ella/Ud.      | -e     | come    |
| nosotros/as      | -emos  | comemos |
| vosotros/as      | -éis   | coméis  |
| ellos/ellas/Uds. | -en    | comen   |

**-ir Verbs (vivir - to live)**

| Subject Pronoun  | Ending | Example |
| ---------------- | ------ | ------- |
| yo               | -o     | vivo    |
| tú               | -es    | vives   |
| él/ella/Ud.      | -e     | vive    |
| nosotros/as      | -imos  | vivimos |
| vosotros/as      | -ís    | vivís   |
| ellos/ellas/Uds. | -en    | viven   |

### Key Patterns to Remember

**📌 yo Form**

- **All verbs** end in **-o** regardless of type
- hablar → hablo, comer → como, vivir → vivo

**📌 tú Form**  

- **-ar verbs**: add **-as** (hablas)
- **-er/-ir verbs**: add **-es** (comes, vives)

**📌 él/ella/Ud. Form**

- **-ar verbs**: add **-a** (habla)
- **-er/-ir verbs**: add **-e** (come, vive)

**📌 nosotros/as Form**

- **-ar verbs**: add **-amos** (hablamos)
- **-er verbs**: add **-emos** (comemos)
- **-ir verbs**: add **-imos** (vivimos)

**📌 vosotros/as Form (Spain only)**

- **-ar verbs**: add **-áis** (habláis)
- **-er verbs**: add **-éis** (coméis)
- **-ir verbs**: add **-ís** (vivís)

**📌 ellos/ellas/Uds. Form**

- **-ar verbs**: add **-an** (hablan)
- **-er/-ir verbs**: add **-en** (comen, viven)

### Three Meanings of Present Tense

Spanish present tense expresses **three different meanings** in English:

**Yo hablo inglés**

- I speak English
- I do speak English  
- I am speaking English

**Tú comes pan**

- You eat bread
- You do eat bread
- You are eating bread

### When to Use Subject Pronouns

**Usually Optional**

Spanish verb endings indicate who performs the action, so pronouns are often unnecessary:

- **Hablo español.** (yo is not necessary)
- **Comemos carne.** (nosotros is not necessary)  
- **Vives en México.** (tú is not necessary)

**Used for Clarification**

When the verb form is ambiguous:

- **Ud. habla** / **él habla** / **ella habla** (all use "habla")

**Used for Emphasis**

To contrast or emphasize:

- **Ella habla español y yo hablo inglés.**
- **Yo como pan y tú comes arroz.**

### Using Two Verbs Together

Like English, you can use two verbs in sequence:

- First verb: **conjugated**
- Second verb: **infinitive form**

**Examples:**

- **Necesito hablar** con Alfredo. (I need to speak with Alfredo)
- **Deseas vivir** en España. (You wish to live in Spain)

## 🔹 Ser vs Estar - The Two "To Be" Verbs

Spanish has **two verbs** that both mean "to be": **ser** and **estar**. Understanding when to use each one is crucial for Spanish learners.

### Basic Difference

**SER** = What something **IS** (essence, identity, characteristics)  
**ESTAR** = How something **IS** (condition, state, location)

**Key Question:**

- **What** is it? → Use **SER**
- **How** is it? → Use **ESTAR**

### Conjugation

**ser (to be)**

| Subject Pronoun  | Conjugation |
| ---------------- | ----------- |
| yo               | soy         |
| tú               | eres        |
| él/ella/Ud.      | es          |
| nosotros/as      | somos       |
| vosotros/as      | sois        |
| ellos/ellas/Uds. | son         |

**estar (to be)**

| Subject Pronoun  | Conjugation |
| ---------------- | ----------- |
| yo               | estoy       |
| tú               | estás       |
| él/ella/Ud.      | está        |
| nosotros/as      | estamos     |
| vosotros/as      | estáis      |
| ellos/ellas/Uds. | están       |

### When to Use SER

**Remember: SER = Essence, Identity, What something IS**

**🕐 Time, Date, Day**

- **¿Qué hora es?** - What time is it?
- **Son las dos.** - It's two o'clock.
- **Hoy es lunes.** - Today is Monday.
- **Es el cinco de mayo.** - It's May fifth.

**🌍 Origin (Where from)**

- **¿De dónde eres?** - Where are you from?
- **Soy de Colombia.** - I'm from Colombia.

**👔 Occupation & Profession**

- **Soy carpintero.** - I'm a carpenter.
- **Es médico.** - He's a doctor.

**🏁 Nationality**

- **¿Es ella puertorriqueña?** - Is she Puerto Rican?
- **Es guatemalteca.** - She's Guatemalan.

**⛪ Religion & Political Affiliation**

- **Son católicos.** - They're Catholics.
- **Es socialista.** - It's socialist.

**🪵 Material (Made of)**

- **¿De qué es la mesa?** - What's the table made of?
- **Es de madera.** - It's made of wood.

**👥 Possession & Relationships**

- **¿De quién es la pluma?** - Whose pen is it?
- **Es de Emilio.** - It's Emilio's.
- **Es el esposo de Marta.** - He's Martha's husband.

**📍 Events (Where taking place)**

- **La fiesta es en mi casa.** - The party is at my house.
- **La película es en el cine.** - The movie is at the theater.

**💭 Impersonal Expressions**

- **Es importante practicar.** - It's important to practice.
- **Es necesario estudiar.** - It's necessary to study.

**✨ Essential Characteristics (Personality, inherent traits)**

- **Miguel es sincero.** - Mike is sincere.
- **Es muy inteligente.** - He's very intelligent.

**➕ When followed by a NOUN**

- **Juan es médico.** - John is a doctor. (Juan = doctor)

### When to Use ESTAR

**Remember: ESTAR = Condition, State, How something IS**

**📍 Location (Where something is located)**

- **¿Dónde estás?** - Where are you?
- **Estoy en casa.** - I'm at home.
- **Chile está en América del Sur.** - Chile is in South America.

**🌡️ Condition & State (Temporary)**

- **¿Cómo está la sopa?** - How's the soup?
- **La sopa está fría.** - The soup is cold.
- **Estoy muy bien.** - I'm very well.

**🏃 Progressive Tenses (Ongoing actions)**

- **¿Qué estás comiendo?** - What are you eating?
- **Estoy estudiando español.** - I'm studying Spanish.

**🗣️ Common Idiomatic Expressions**

- **estar de acuerdo** - to be in agreement
- **estar de pie** - to be standing
- **estar en camino** - to be on the way
- **estar en las nubes** - to daydream

### SER vs ESTAR with Adjectives

The choice between **ser** and **estar** can **change the meaning** of the same adjective:

| Adjective    | With SER (essence)                | With ESTAR (condition)              |
| ------------ | --------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| **verde**    | Es verde (it's green in color)    | Está verde (it's unripe)            |
| **aburrido** | Es aburrido (he's boring)         | Está aburrido (he's bored)          |
| **listo**    | Es listo (he's smart)             | Está listo (he's ready)             |
| **callado**  | Es callado (he's quiet by nature) | Está callado (he's being quiet now) |

**Examples:**

**La manzana es verde.** - The apple is green (in color).  
**La manzana está verde.** - The apple is green (unripe).

**El profesor es aburrido.** - The professor is boring.  
**El profesor está aburrido.** - The professor is bored.

### Location vs Origin vs Events

This is a common confusion area. Here's how to distinguish:

**Origin (where from)** → **SER**

- **Mónica es de España.** - Monica is from Spain.

**Current Location (where now)** → **ESTAR**

- **Mónica está en Venezuela.** - Monica is in Venezuela.

**Event Location (where taking place)** → **SER**

- **La fiesta es en la casa de Mónica.** - The party is at Monica's house.

### Quick Decision Rules

**Use SER when:**

1. **Noun follows the verb** → Juan **es** médico
2. **Adjective describes essence** → Es muy **inteligente**
3. **Time, date, day** → **Son** las tres
4. **Origin** → **Soy** de México
5. **Events taking place** → La clase **es** en el aula

**Use ESTAR when:**

1. **Location** → **Estoy** en casa
2. **Adjective describes condition** → **Está** enfermo
3. **Progressive tenses** → **Estoy** comiendo
4. **Temporary states** → **Está** cerrado

> **💡 Memory Tip:** Think of **SER** as "**S**erious" (permanent, essential) and **ESTAR** as "**E**verything **T**emporary" (conditions, locations, states that can change).

## 🔹 Stem-Changing Verbs

### What Are Stem-Changing Verbs?

Unlike regular verbs where only the ending changes, **stem-changing verbs** also change their stem when conjugated. The stem is the part of the infinitive that remains after removing -ar, -er, or -ir.

**Example:**

- **hablar** → stem: *habl* + ending: *ar*
- **comer** → stem: *com* + ending: *er*  
- **vivir** → stem: *viv* + ending: *ir*

**Key Rule:** The stem change occurs in **all forms except nosotros and vosotros**.

### Three Types of Stem Changes

**o → ue** (boot verbs)

- The letter **o** in the stem changes to **ue**
- Example: *contar* → *cuento*, *cuentas*, *cuenta*

**e → ie** (boot verbs)

- The letter **e** in the stem changes to **ie**
- Example: *cerrar* → *cierro*, *cierras*, *cierra*

**e → i** (only -ir verbs)

- The letter **e** in the stem changes to **i**
- Example: *repetir* → *repito*, *repites*, *repite*

### Important Notes

**"Boot" Pattern**

- Stem-changing verbs are often called "boot verbs" because when you draw a line around the forms that change, it looks like a boot
- The **nosotros** and **vosotros** forms always keep the original stem

**e → i Changes Only Apply to -ir Verbs**

- This pattern is exclusive to -ir verbs
- -ar and -er verbs never follow the e → i pattern

**Special Irregular Cases**

- **decir** (to say): *yo digo* (not "yo dico")
- **bendecir** and **maldecir** also have irregular yo forms

**Endings Remain Regular**

- Only the stem changes; the endings follow normal patterns
- -ar verbs still use -ar endings, -er verbs use -er endings, etc.

> **💡 Study Tip:** Practice with the "boot" visual - imagine drawing a boot around the forms that change (yo, tú, él/ella/Ud., ellos/ellas/Uds.) while nosotros and vosotros stay outside the boot with their original stems.
